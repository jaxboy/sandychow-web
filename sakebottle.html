Packaging Design: Saké Bottle

Saké is Japan's most popular beer and has been more popular in the U.S. when it reached our shore in the 1990s. Saké is known as Japanese rice wine but it's not exactly wine. It's actually beer. Saké has been broken down in a two-step fermentation process. Rice starch is converted to sugar, then the sugar is converted to alcohol by yeast making the saké production process similar to beer than wine.

The more polish the rice is, the higher the grade of saké. Saké can be served hot or cold. As cold saké grew in popularity, people assumed that saké served hot was lower in quality and cold saké was higher quality. However, it depends on the quality and picking the right saké for the right temperature will change the quality of it.

The Objective

To design 3 saké bottle packaging in different flavors

The Solution

The design of the bottle and color corresponds to the word in traditional Chinese calligraphy.

  • 1) 山 (mountain) The color is shown blue which indicates winter and icy cold snow in Fiji Mountain.‭
  • 2) 春 (spring)The color is pink which indicates cherry blossoms thats grows in the spring time and this was dedicated to the Cherry Blossom Festival.
  • 3) 水 (water) This is a clear color and this design approach is to integrate 水 and the fishes to create the movement of water and create a ripple effect. The chinese character for water has a very unique sense of movement almost like water.
